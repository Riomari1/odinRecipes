Japanese Oden

Ingredients

  • Ganmodoki (boiled)
  • Konjac / Konnyaku blocks (sliced into a large triangle shape)
  • Shirataki noodles
  • Wiener sausages – sub with Frankfurts
  • Bok Choy
  • Carrot
  • Potato
  • Mushroom, enoki, shimeji or shiitake work best
  • Tsukune meatballs
  • Assorted fish cakes / surimi shapes (satsuma-age)
  • Atsuage or aburaage
  • Boneless chicken thighs
  • Pork belly slices
  • Tsumire meatballs
  • Tofu – fried, puffs or firm work best
  • Dashi Stock Powder
  • Soy Sauce
  • Mirin
  • Cooking Sake

Steps

  1. Peel the daikon and cut into thick 2 cm / 1 in chunks.
  2. Peel the top and bottom edges of each piece to "round them off" (this will prevent them from breaking while cooking).
  3. Make two cross slits in each daikon piece around a third / half way through.
  4. Place each piece (cross side down) into a medium saucepan with the rice and cover with water.
  5. Drain the daikon, discarding the water and rice.
  6. Pour boiling water over fish cakes to remove oil.
  7. Put edamame beans onto a skewer.
  8. Place a boiled egg into the oden broth.
  9. Prepare your oden ingredients, such as fish cakes, fish balls, chikuwa, etc. Pour boiling water over them to remove some of the oil. Drain and optionally slice them in half on a diagonal and put them on wooden skewers.
  10. If using edamame beans, place around 5 on each small wooden skewer.
  11. Peel and chop any other optional ingredients and get them ready.
  12. Pour dashi stock, soy sauce, mirin, and cooking sake into a large pot and bring to a boil, then reduce to a simmer.
  13. Add daikon and boiled eggs to the broth and continue to simmer for 20 minutes.
  14. Add remaining oden ingredients, such as edamame bean skewers, chikuwa, and fish cakes, and continue to simmer for a further 30 minutes.
  15. Turn off the heat and cover the pot. Allow the oden to stand for an hour to let the broth infuse with the ingredients.
  16. Once cooled, refrigerate for maximum flavor or reheat at a simmer for 15-20 minutes and serve with Japanese kurashi mustard and shichimi togarashi.
